Defining and Distinguishing the Problems:
The Job Grouping Problem (JGP) (also known as the Tool Switching Instants Problem): This problem aims to partition a set of jobs into a minimum number of feasible groups (or batches) such that the tools required for all jobs in a group fit within the magazine capacity b (or C). The goal here is to minimize the number of complete machine setups or stops
.
The Tool Switching Problem (SSP): In contrast, the SSP assumes jobs are processed sequentially. It involves determining the job sequence and the specific tools present in the magazine for each job to minimize the total number of individual tool switches
.
SSP Decomposition & The Baseline: Explain that the SSP can be decomposed into a Sequencing Problem (NP-hard) and a Tooling Problem
. Introduce the Keep Tool Needed Soonest (KTNS) policy, establishing that if the job sequence is fixed, KTNS optimally solves the Tooling Problem in polynomial time by removing the tools whose next required use is furthest in the future
.
